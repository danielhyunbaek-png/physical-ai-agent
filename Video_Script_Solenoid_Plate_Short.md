# Short-form script (Mark Rober style) — "I built a robot hand that types" (Part X)

**Platform:** Instagram Reels / X · **Format:** 9:16 vertical · **Length:** ~80s · **Audio:** VO over b-roll + face shots
**Voice:** curious, excited, talks-with-his-hands. Pose a problem, hit a snag, drop a big satisfying number, pay it off. Captions burned in (assume muted), cut every 1.5–3s.

---

## HOOK — 0:00–0:05  *(face shot, hold a single solenoid)*

- **B-roll:** You, close, grinning, holding one solenoid like it's a tiny treasure.
- **On-screen text:** `Why would I do this??`
- **VO:** "Okay, so I just spent 350 bucks building a robot whose entire job is to press keys on a keyboard. And no — I couldn't just use software. Stick with me, because this is way cooler than it sounds."

## SETUP — 0:05–0:16  *(pile / unboxing — TO SHOOT)*

- **B-roll:** Dump all 100 solenoids into frame, slow-mo. Pile fills the screen.
- **On-screen text:** `100 robotic fingers 🦾`
- **VO:** "Meet the star of the show: the solenoid. Think of it as a tiny robotic finger — give it electricity, and it punches. I needed one for every single key… so naturally, I bought a hundred of 'em."

## HOW IT WORKS — 0:16–0:29  *(firing motion diagram — slow it down)*

- **B-roll:** Screen-record of the firing cross-section, idle → energize → press. Punch the "thunk" SFX on contact.
- **On-screen text:** `magnet → SNAP → keystroke`
- **VO:** "And here's the genius part. Inside is a coil of wire. Power it up, it turns into a magnet and yanks this steel rod down like a mousetrap. A little rubber tip taps the key, a spring snaps it back — and boom. One perfect keystroke."

## THE PROBLEM — 0:29–0:42  *(CAD — HAVE)*

- **B-roll:** Fusion 360 — spin the plate, then exaggerate the tilt, show the flat-vs-tilted gap.
- **On-screen text:** `Problem: keyboards aren't flat 😬`
- **VO:** "But then I hit a wall. Keyboards aren't flat — yours is tilted about four degrees right now. So a flat plate of fingers? The front keys would never get touched. The fix: tilt the whole plate to match, so every finger has the exact same reach. Boom."

## ASSEMBLY — 0:42–0:55  *(mounting solenoids — TO SHOOT)*

- **B-roll:** Fast, satisfying cuts of screwing solenoids in, a row filling up. End on a punch-in of the last screw.
- **On-screen text:** `168 screws. send help.`
- **VO:** "Then came the *fun* part. And by fun, I mean mounting all 84 of these by hand. Two screws each — that's a hundred and sixty-eight tiny screws. My thumb may never recover."

## THE TEST — 0:55–1:08  *(testing the 84 — HAVE)*

- **B-roll:** Solenoids snapping on the bench, build to your most satisfying firing clip. Land the loaded plate.
- **On-screen text:** `will ALL 84 fire?? 👀`
- **VO:** "Moment of truth. These are dirt-cheap bulk parts, so some of 'em are basically guaranteed to be duds. So I tested every… single… one — to find 84 keepers." *(beat — let the best clip breathe)*

## PAYOFF + TEASE — 1:08–1:20  *(hero shot → face)*

- **B-roll:** Slow pan across the fully loaded plate. Cut to your face, satisfied.
- **On-screen text:** `Part X → making it type on command`
- **VO:** "And… there she is. A robotic hand that types. Next up, I wire all 84 to a brain so it can type literally anything I tell it to. Smash that follow — because this is about to get wild."

## Production notes (the Mark Rober touches)
- **Analogy first, jargon never:** "robotic finger," "mousetrap," "give it a brain" — never say "actuator" or "GPIO" out loud. The diagram carries the technical truth; the VO keeps it fun.
- **The problem→fix beat (0:29) is the heart of the style.** Don't cut it. The tilt reveal is your "ohhh, clever" moment — the thing the hater's reaction videos can't fake because you actually solved it.
- **Big-number spectacle:** let "168 screws" and "100 of 'em" land with a beat and a text pop. Rober always lets the absurd number breathe.
- **Sound design is half the video:** punch the solenoid "thunk" on the firing diagram and every test clip. That snap is your signature sound.
- **Two hook options:** (A) the $350 line above; (B) cold-open on the firing diagram looping + "There's no hand pressing this key. Watch." Test both, keep the higher-retention one.
- **Captions:** big, high-contrast, upper-third (dodge the IG UI). Burned in.
- **Loop it:** last frame (loaded plate) should rhyme with the opening pile so a replay feels seamless.
