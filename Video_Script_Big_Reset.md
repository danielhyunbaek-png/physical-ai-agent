## Script

**[HOOK — 0:00–0:03]**
*Overhead or wide shot of the fully populated 84-solenoid plate, wire mess and all. Then face.*

> "This plate has 84 solenoids on it. It works. And I'm tearing the entire thing apart."

**[THE FIRST WALL — 0:03–0:15]**
*Cut to the cell #1 soldering clip (footage on hand). Speed-ramp it. Then hold on the finished perfboard cell.*

> "Here's why. This is one driver cell — it controls 8 keys. Hand-soldering it took me four hours. I need eleven of them. Plus 84 more wire connections after that. That's 25-plus hours of this."

**[PIVOT 1: THE PCB — 0:15–0:27]**
*Screen recording: EasyEDA layout, then the JLCPCB order confirmation. On-screen graphic: "25+ hours" crossed out → "$40, 2 weeks."*

> "So I did what actual engineers do: designed a custom circuit board. Every one of those hand-soldered wires becomes a copper trace. Forty bucks. Two weeks. Done."

**[PIVOT 2: THE PLATE — 0:27–0:42]**
*Cut to the plate. Point at one solenoid in the middle of an interior row. Slow zoom or finger tap.*

> "And while the boards ship — the plate gets redesigned too. See this solenoid in the middle? If it dies, I have to unscrew every row behind it to reach it. The new design: removable walls, the circuit boards mount on a deck right on top, and every wire routes straight up instead of… whatever this is."

*Gesture at the wire tangle. Let it get a beat of silence. It's funny.*

**[THE LESSON + CTA — 0:42–0:55]**
*Face cam, dry, confident.*

> "168 screws are coming out of a thing I just built. Week [X] and I'm doing my biggest teardown yet — on purpose. Sometimes the fastest way forward is backwards. Boards land in two weeks. Follow for the rebuild."
