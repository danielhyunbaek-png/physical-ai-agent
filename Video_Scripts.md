## VIDEO 1: The Launch / Concept Video
**Title:** "I'm building a robot that physically types on a keyboard"
**Platform:** All 4 (TikTok, Reels, Shorts, X)
**Length:** 45–60 seconds
**When to post:** NOW / Week 1, before parts even arrive

---

### Script

**[HOOK — 0:00–0:03]**
*On camera, holding up the Nuphy Air75 keyboard*

> "This keyboard is about to have 84 tiny robots sitting on top of it — one for every single key."

**[CONTEXT — 0:03–0:12]**
*Cut to: diagram or phone sketch of the concept. Or just talk to camera.*

> "I'm building a physical AI agent. Not software — an actual robot that presses real keys on a real keyboard. 84 solenoids. Individually wired. It'll be able to type anything the AI tells it to."

**[THE QUESTION — 0:12–0:22]**
*Back to face, slightly leaning in*

> "Yeah, I know — why not just use software? Here's the thing: software AI agents only work with apps that have APIs. A physical agent can use *any* computer. Anything a human can use. Because it's using the same hands."

**[STAKES / TIMELINE — 0:22–0:35]**
*Show the parts spreadsheet briefly, or a printed list. Casual.*

> "I've got 12 weeks and a $1,366 budget. Parts are ordered. The math says I need to fire each solenoid in about 20 milliseconds or the typing will sound like a drunk person. There's a lot that can go wrong."

**[CTA — 0:35–0:45]**
*Smile, direct to camera*

> "I'm posting every week. Week 3 I'll fire the first solenoid. Week 12 the robot types its first real sentence. Follow along — this is either going to be incredible or a very expensive pile of magnets."

---

### Shot List
- [ ] You on camera, holding keyboard (phone on tripod, eye level)
- [ ] Close-up of keyboard keys (overhead or side angle)
- [ ] Screen recording or quick sketch of the concept layout (84 solenoids above keys)
- [ ] Quick flash of the spreadsheet or parts list (just 1–2 seconds)
- [ ] Outro face shot — casual, confident

### Edit Notes
- Cut snappy. No shot should linger more than 3 seconds in the middle section.
- Captions on every line (use CapCut auto-caption, clean up errors)
- Add a subtle mechanical/typing sound effect under the "84 solenoids" line
- B-roll idea: timelapse of the spreadsheet scrolling (shows scope/seriousness)
- Color grade: slightly warm. You're in a workshop, not a corporate office.
- End card: "Week 2 coming [day]" with a preview frame

## VIDEO 2: The Explainer
**Title:** "Why does AI need to physically type?"
**Platform:** All 4
**Length:** 45–55 seconds
**When to post:** Week 1–2

---

### Script

**[HOOK — 0:00–0:03]**
*Direct to camera, slightly skeptical face*

> "Everyone's first question when I tell them this project: 'Why not just use software?'"

**[ANSWER — 0:03–0:25]**
*Cut between face and quick diagram/whiteboard sketch*

> "Software AI agents — like the ones you use in ChatGPT or Cursor — only work with apps that have an API. They need the software to cooperate.

> A physical agent doesn't need permission. It sees the screen through a camera, decides what to click or type, and just... does it. On *any* app. A hospital's 30-year-old software with no API. A game that blocks automation. A web page that breaks every bot.

> It uses the same interface a human uses — a keyboard and a mouse."

**[THE TWIST — 0:25–0:38]**
*Lean in slightly, slower pace*

> "That's actually why this is hard. Software is perfect. My robot has to deal with physical reality — tolerance errors, solenoid timing, OCR misreads. But that's also what makes it interesting."

**[CTA — 0:38–0:50]**

> "I'm 12 weeks into building one from scratch. Come back Friday — the parts just arrived."

---

### Shot List
- [ ] Face shot, slightly skeptical expression for hook
- [ ] Whiteboard or iPad sketch: "Software AI" with locked door vs "Physical AI" with real keyboard
- [ ] Screen recording: ChatGPT or Cursor UI (just the concept, no need for demo)
- [ ] Close-up of keyboard + your hand pressing a key naturally
- [ ] Face shot for closer/CTA

### Edit Notes
- The whiteboard sketch can be hand-drawn on paper and filmed overhead — don't overthink it
- This video should feel slightly more "educational YouTube" than the others — slightly slower pace than Video 1
- Text overlay on "any app" line: flash 3 icons (random apps) in quick succession
- Save-worthy content: people will share this explanation to answer "why" on your behalf. Make the explanation genuinely clear.

## VIDEO 3: The Unboxing
**Title:** "100 solenoids just arrived from China"
**Platform:** All 4
**Length:** 30–45 seconds
**When to post:** Week 2–3, day parts arrive

---

### Script

**[HOOK — 0:00–0:02]**
*Film the box arriving. If you can't film the delivery, film yourself cutting it open.*

> *(No talking — just the sound of tape cutting)*

**[THE REVEAL — 0:02–0:10]**
*Pull out the solenoids. One handful. Show quantity.*

> "One hundred solenoids. Each one wired to a different key on a keyboard. They need to fire in about 20 milliseconds each."

**[CLOSE-UP — 0:10–0:20]**
*Hold one up, click it manually with your finger*

> "Each one is basically a tiny electromagnet. When current hits it — [demonstrate firing with battery/hand] — the plunger shoots down. That's the keystroke."

*(Let the click sound carry. Don't talk over it.)*

**[THE SCALE — 0:20–0:30]**
*Pour or spread them all on the desk. Show the pile.*

> "I need 84 of these mounted, wired, and positioned over every single key. All at the same height. All firing in sync."

**[CTA — 0:30–0:40]**

> "Week 3 I fire the first one. Follow to see if I survive the wiring."

---

### Shot List
- [ ] Box arriving or being carried to desk (even just pulling it from inside)
- [ ] Cutting the box open (overhead angle)
- [ ] Pulling out solenoids — show the quantity
- [ ] Close-up of single solenoid, rotating it in your hand
- [ ] Manually triggering one (attach a 9V battery to the leads — it WILL fire and it's very satisfying)
- [ ] Overhead shot: all solenoids spread on the desk or table

### Edit Notes
- **THE BEST SHOT IN THIS VIDEO:** manually trigger a single solenoid with a 9V battery close-up. The click sound and the physical snap of the plunger is extremely satisfying and will drive re-watches.
- Slow motion on the manual trigger if your phone supports it (0.5x is fine)
- Keep it raw. Don't over-edit this one. The realness is the point.
- Sound design: amplify the click sounds slightly. Don't add music under the demonstration sections.
- Consider cutting this to 2 versions: 30sec "reel" version and a slightly longer 60sec "explainer" version for YouTube Shorts.

## VIDEO 4: First Keystroke
**Title:** "Day 21: The first key press"
**Platform:** All 4
**Length:** 45–60 seconds
**When to post:** Week 3, after 4×4 test plate success

---

### Script

**[HOOK — 0:00–0:03]**
*Show the result first: a letter appearing on screen, pressed by the solenoid*

> "Day 21. First keystroke."

*(Let the solenoid click play. Hold on the screen showing the letter.)*

**[CONTEXT — 0:03–0:15]**
*Cut back to the test setup*

> "I printed a 4×4 test plate — 16 solenoids, just enough to type a 4-letter word. The plate took 6 hours to print. The mounting took 3 more. And then I held my breath and sent the first signal."

**[THE STRUGGLE — 0:15–0:30]**
*Be honest about what went wrong first*

> "First attempt? [Describe what went wrong — wrong key, misfire, too slow, etc.] [Second attempt] The solenoid fired but the plunger missed the key by 2mm. [Third attempt] I shimmed the plate up by 1.5mm and—"

*Cut to the successful keystroke. Let it land. Slow motion.*

**[REACTION — 0:30–0:40]**
*Face cam, genuine*

> "It's one keystroke. It's one letter. But it means the whole concept works. Scale this up by 83 and I have a full keyboard."

**[CTA — 0:40–0:55]**

> "Next week: all 84 keys wired. If you thought one solenoid was satisfying—"

*Cut to a quick preview of the full matrix (even if it's just the empty plate)*

> "—wait."

---

### Shot List
- [ ] The money shot FIRST: solenoid pressing a key, letter appearing on screen (film this multiple times from different angles before anything else)
- [ ] 3D printed 4×4 test plate in your hands (before mounting)
- [ ] Time-lapse or fast-cut of mounting the solenoids
- [ ] The first failed attempt (you want this footage — it makes success feel earned)
- [ ] Slow-motion close-up of the solenoid firing and hitting the key
- [ ] Screen with the letter typed
- [ ] Your face reaction (genuine — don't fake-react)
- [ ] Preview shot of the full keyboard/empty 84-key plate

### Edit Notes
- **START with the success.** Don't make them wait. Show the letter appearing in the first 3 seconds, THEN walk back to how you got there.
- Slow motion is essential for the keystroke moment. Film at the highest frame rate your phone allows.
- Sound design: this video's audio is the star. The click of the solenoid, the key activating — leave it clean.
- Add a simple counter graphic if possible: "Keystrokes: 1 / 84"
- This video has the highest viral potential of the first 5. Post it when you have the most eyes on your account.

## VIDEO 5: The Struggle / Pivot Moment
**Title:** "I ordered 168 wrong screws"
**Platform:** All 4
**Length:** 30–45 seconds
**When to post:** Week 2–4, whenever a genuine mistake happens

*(This script is built around the M2 vs M2.5 screw discovery — adapt details to whatever mistake actually happens)*

---

### Script

**[HOOK — 0:00–0:03]**
*Hold up the wrong screws. Or just talk to camera with "oh no" energy.*

> "I ordered 168 screws. They're the wrong size."

**[EXPLAIN — 0:03–0:18]**

> "The solenoid mounting holes are 2.5mm. I ordered M2 screws — which are 2.0mm. That's a 0.5mm gap on every single mounting point. On a machine that needs to fire in 20 milliseconds with consistent positioning across 84 keys? That's not acceptable."

**[THE FIX — 0:18–0:30]**

> "Reordered M2.5, 8mm length. Also got heat-set inserts as backup — cleaner assembly anyway. Total damage: $12 and 6 days of shipping."

**[THE LESSON — 0:30–0:40]**
*Dry humor, matter-of-fact*

> "Week 2 of the build and I've already made a mistake that cost me a week. Totally fine. Totally normal. This is going great."

---

### Shot List
- [ ] Wrong screws in hand (or next to the solenoid to show the size mismatch)
- [ ] Close-up showing M2 screw vs M2.5 screw next to each other (if you have both)
- [ ] Screenshot of the reorder confirmation (blur out personal info)
- [ ] Face cam: dry humor delivery on final line

### Edit Notes
- Lean into the dry humor. Don't be overly upset — confident acceptance of the setback is funnier and more watchable than frustration.
- Use a simple on-screen graphic: "M2 = 2.0mm" crossed out → "M2.5 = 2.5mm" checkmark
- This video will perform well because it's **relatable**. Every person who's ever done DIY has ordered the wrong part. It's validation content.
- Comments will be full of people with their own wrong-part stories. Reply to them. This is how you build a community in week 2.

## Universal Rules for All Videos

**The first 2 seconds determine everything.** Start with the most visually interesting or surprising thing. Never start with "Hey guys" or "So today I'm going to be."

**Let good sounds play.** When a solenoid clicks, a print finishes, or a key types — leave space for the audio. Don't talk over it.

**Be specific.** Every number you say ("20 milliseconds," "84 solenoids," "$1,366") increases credibility and shareability. Vague build videos are boring. Precise ones are watchable.

**Show your face.** Even if it's just 5 seconds. People follow people, not projects.

**End with motion.** Either a result, a cliffhanger ("next week I wire all 84"), or a question ("what should the robot type first?"). Never end with the video just stopping.
