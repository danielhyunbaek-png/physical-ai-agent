# Video 4 — B-Roll Shot List (2×2 First Keystroke)

**Video:** "Day [X]: The first key press" — updated for the **2×2** prototype (front S/D, back W/E).
**Why 2×2 not 4×4:** it forces a two-row, *tilted (4°)*, *staggered (4.76mm)* mount — the two hardest parts of the whole build. That's your story.
**Golden rule:** film the success shot FIRST, multiple angles, before anything can break. Everything else is recoverable; the first clean keystroke is not.

---

## Phase 1 — CAD / Design (you're here now)
- [ ] **Screen recording: the parametric model rotating** in Fusion 360 — show the plate, the two mounting walls, the plunger holes.
- [ ] **Screen recording: change a parameter live** (e.g. `tilt_angle` or `pitch_x`) and let the whole model update. This is the "wow, it's parametric" beat.
- [ ] **Close-up of the tilt** — orbit to a side view so the 4° angle and the back-row-taller geometry is obvious.
- [ ] **Screen recording: the stagger** — top view showing the back row (W/E) offset left of the front row (S/D).
- [ ] **You at the keyboard, calipers in hand**, measuring key pitch (19.05mm) — establishes that this is measured, not guessed.
- [ ] **Hand on mouse / face lit by monitor** — a few seconds of "designing" ambiance for cutaways.

## Phase 2 — The Test-Fit Cell (the smart-engineer beat)
- [ ] **The single test-fit cell coming off the print bed** (one hole + one wall) — this is your "measure twice" move; it's relatable and makes you look careful.
- [ ] **Dropping one solenoid into the test cell** to check `wall_offset` — does it sit flush?
- [ ] **Close-up: caliper checking the plunger-to-key gap** on the test cell.
- [ ] *(Optional honesty beat)* if the wall_offset is wrong on the first cell — **film the reprint**. Failure footage makes the success earned.

## Phase 3 — Printing the 2×2 Plate
- [ ] **Time-lapse of the full 2×2 plate printing** (phone on tripod, locked off, shoot the whole print).
- [ ] **First layer going down** — close, low angle across the bed. Always a good clip.
- [ ] **Peeling the finished plate off the bed** — satisfying, tactile.
- [ ] **The plate + mounting walls held up in your hand**, turning it to show the holes and the angle.

## Phase 4 — Mounting & Assembly
- [ ] **Fast-cut / time-lapse of mounting the 4 solenoids** to the walls (M2.5 screws, side-face holes).
- [ ] **Macro: capping a plunger with silicone tubing** — tiny, detailed, very watchable.
- [ ] **Macro: driving an M2.5 screw** into the wall (the screw you re-ordered — callback to the "wrong screws" video).
- [ ] **The plate lowering onto the keyboard**, over W/E/S/D — overhead.
- [ ] **Shimming the plate to the 1–2mm gap** — caliper or feeler check between plunger tip and keycap.
- [ ] **Side-on hero of the finished rig**: 4 solenoids standing on their tilted walls over the keyboard. Hold this — it's your thumbnail candidate.

## Phase 5 — THE MONEY SHOTS (film these the most)
- [ ] **Solenoid fires → letter appears on screen.** Get it from 3+ angles: (1) macro on the plunger hitting the key, (2) the laptop screen, (3) a wide of rig + screen together.
- [ ] **Slow-motion of one plunger firing and striking the keycap** — highest frame rate your phone allows.
- [ ] **Screen close-up as the letters land** — the 2×2 gives you W, E, S, D. Fire them to spell a word ("**WEDS**" / "**SEW**" / "**DEW**") instead of random order; spelling something reads as success.
- [ ] **All four firing in sequence** — a little click-wave, clean audio, no music.
- [ ] **Macro from directly above** a key, plunger entering frame and pressing down.

## Phase 6 — Reaction & Wrap
- [ ] **Your genuine face reaction** to the first clean keystroke (don't fake it — keep the camera rolling during the real attempt).
- [ ] **Talking-head to camera** for the "one keystroke, but the concept works — scale by 21x" line.
- [ ] **Preview shot for next week**: the empty/future 84-key plate, or you holding the keyboard and gesturing at all the keys still to cover.

## Texture / cutaway B-roll (grab whenever, fills edit gaps)
- [ ] Desk/bench wide establishing shot.
- [ ] Pile of spare solenoids.
- [ ] The spreadsheet or CAD brief on screen (1–2 sec scope flash).
- [ ] Hands working — screwdriver, calipers, tweezers — shallow depth of field.
- [ ] The keyboard alone, clean, before the rig goes on (the "before").
